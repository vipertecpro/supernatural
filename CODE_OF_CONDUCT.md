# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

Project spaces should be welcoming, constructive, and safe. Participants must communicate respectfully, accept good-faith feedback, respect boundaries and attribution, and focus criticism on work rather than people.

Unacceptable conduct includes harassment, hate speech, threats, stalking, sexual harassment, discriminatory language, doxxing, impersonation, deliberate misgendering, spam, disruption, or publishing private communications or personal information without permission.

Fandom disagreement, canon interpretation, and critique never justify personal attacks. Spoilers must be handled according to the project's content rules.

## Scope and Enforcement

This standard applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, project chat, events, and public representation of the project. Maintainers may edit or remove content, warn participants, restrict participation, or permanently ban accounts based on severity and pattern.

Report conduct concerns privately through an available repository-owner contact channel. Do not post sensitive allegations or personal information publicly. Maintainers should limit access to reports, avoid retaliation, consider conflicts of interest, and communicate outcomes only to the extent appropriate for safety and privacy.

Enforcement decisions may be appealed once with concise new context. Appeals do not permit continued harmful behavior while under review.
