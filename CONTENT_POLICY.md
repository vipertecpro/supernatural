# Content Policy

This foundation policy is not a substitute for legal review. It defines the minimum expectations for future content and community work.

## Rights, Sources, and Attribution

- Record a canonical source, creator/publisher when known, applicable license, required attribution, and usage notes.
- Do not represent unknown commercial or derivative rights as allowed.
- Contributors must own submitted content or have permission to submit and license it for the intended use.
- Fan art requires creator permission and attribution unless an applicable license clearly permits use.
- Embedding a provider-authorized video is distinct from downloading or rehosting it. Respect provider terms and removal controls.
- Do not rehost episodes, music, protected video, fonts, logos, or other copyrighted media without explicit rights.
- Do not copy or publish full transcripts or substantial protected text. Short quotations must be justified, attributed, and limited.
- Never scrape or bulk-ingest third-party sites without an approved technical, legal, and provider-policy review.
- Hosted uploads remain private until file validation, moderation, attribution, and intended-use rights succeed. Provider embedding permission never grants hosting or download permission.
- Search projections may contain only approved public bounded text; they must be removed when source publication, rights, moderation, takedown, or spoiler visibility no longer permits discovery.

## User-Submitted and Community Content

Future upload and community features must provide ownership assertions, attribution, reporting, moderation, appeal, and removal workflows before launch. Prohibited content includes illegal material, credible threats, harassment, hate speech, sexual exploitation, non-consensual intimate content, doxxing, impersonation, malware, fraud, spam, and repeated infringement.

Sexual content must comply with law, platform rules, age-safety requirements, and clearly defined product boundaries. Sexual content involving minors is prohibited without exception and must be escalated according to applicable law.

## Spoilers

Content must use the spoiler-classification foundation where reveals are material. Human-readable warnings and structured progress constraints should allow future filtering across pages, search, notifications, community content, chat, and AI answers. Deliberately evading spoiler controls may be moderated.

## Moderation and Appeals

Moderators may hide, restrict, label, or remove content; restrict accounts; preserve minimum audit evidence; and escalate safety or rights concerns. Decisions should record a reason without storing unnecessary personal or sensitive data. Users should have a proportionate appeal path. Repeated harassment, spam, impersonation, or infringement may lead to permanent restrictions.

Reports are private to the reporter and authorized case-scoped moderators. A report never proves a violation and never applies enforcement automatically. Subjects must not receive reporter identity, private evidence, or internal notes. Severe/permanent action and rights/takedown decisions require their explicit additional authority. Notification copy must use the same spoiler, content-restriction, and privacy boundary as in-app resources.

See `COPYRIGHT_AND_TAKEDOWN.md` for rights-holder requests.

Prompt 10 Community content is plain text, spoiler-classified, reportable, and rate limited. Local moderators cannot apply platform restrictions or see reporter identity. Private Bunkers, bookmarks, voter identities, invitation tokens, and local private notes must not enter feeds, notifications, or logs.

Personal blocks and mutes are private safety preferences, not public accusations or moderation findings. They do not delete public content or prevent reporting/appeal, and targets must not be notified or told the direction of a block.
