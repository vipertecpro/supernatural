# Copyright and Takedown Process

This document describes a project process, not legal advice or a legally reviewed production policy.

## Submitting a Concern

Rights holders or authorized representatives may open a repository issue containing only non-sensitive notice that a copyright concern exists and request private follow-up from the repository owner. Do not publish home addresses, identity documents, signatures, private contracts, or other sensitive information in an issue.

A complete private notice should identify:

- the copyrighted work or authorized representative;
- the exact repository or application location at issue;
- the basis for the claim and requested action;
- a good-faith statement that the use is not authorized;
- accurate contact information supplied privately;
- any relevant license, permission, or countervailing context.

Security-reporting channels are reserved for vulnerabilities and should not be used for ordinary copyright disputes.

## Project Handling

Maintainers may temporarily disable disputed material while reviewing provenance, licenses, attribution, provider terms, and contributor records. They may remove, replace, restore, or externally reference material based on available evidence. The project will avoid publishing unnecessary personal information about claimants or contributors.

Contributors may provide a private response or counter-notice where legally appropriate. The project does not promise a fixed response time or adjudicate ownership beyond what is necessary to manage repository/application content.

Repeated knowing infringement may result in contribution or account restrictions. Removal of an asset does not necessarily remove factual source/audit records required to understand the action.

Media records under a credible takedown concern must be restricted from public Media resources, attachments, variants, and Search projections while provenance is reviewed. Restoration requires an explicit authorized rights decision; provider availability and embedding permission do not establish hosting rights.
