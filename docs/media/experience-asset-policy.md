# Experience asset policy

Every experience asset must exist in `resources/experience/assets-manifest.ts` before public use. Required fields cover stable key, type, source, source URL, creator/provider, licence or usage basis, attribution, hosting/embedding/derivative/commercial decisions, expiry, delivery type, fallback, visibility, and review status.

Validation fails closed for duplicate keys, unapproved review state, unknown hosting/commercial permission, missing provenance, or a remote asset without a source URL. Private legal notes never belong in the client manifest.

Allowed assets are project-owner uploads with cleared rights, explicitly compatible licences, public-domain/CC0 work, configured TMDB CDN images under accepted terms, official authorized YouTube embeds, approved Media records, and repository-original procedural SVG/Canvas/WebGL/audio. Public availability alone is not permission.

Episodes, downloaded trailers, extracted trailer audio, soundtracks, official logos/symbols/type, actor likeness generation, promotional art without permission, arbitrary fan art, and hotlinked media are prohibited.
