# Product Experience Principles

Working title: **The Archive**. This is a codename, not an approved product name.

## Experience contract

1. **Clarity before atmosphere.** Location, next action, visibility, privacy, spoiler state, draft state, and restrictions are expressed in text and structure before decoration. Atmosphere may frame a decision; it never becomes the decision control.
2. **Cinematic at the threshold, practical in the work.** Public marketing and selected universe landings may tell visual stories. Search, reading, progress, Community, settings, and every workspace optimize for speed and comprehension.
3. **The archive is evidence-led.** Catalog facts, Lore assertions, citations, rights state, editorial status, and moderation state are distinguishable. Fan interpretation never masquerades as reviewed canon.
4. **Personal means private by default.** Journey, notes, bookmarks, ratings, notifications, block/mute lists, and private memberships are labelled private at entry and never used as public decoration.
5. **Spoiler safety is a coherent product mode.** Visible, warning, redacted, and hidden are intentional states with consistent vocabulary. Sensitive text is absent, not merely blurred, when the server withholds it.
6. **Authority is local and legible.** Platform permissions, contributor authority, moderator authority, and Bunker-local roles have separate labels and surfaces. Ordinary users see human terms, never permission keys.
7. **Progressive enhancement, never progressive exclusion.** Base HTML and static visuals support every essential path. Enhanced motion and immersive rendering are replaceable layers.
8. **Recovery over dead ends.** Empty, offline, rate-limited, conflict, archived, removed, and restricted states explain the safe next action without leaking protected facts.
9. **Original fandom-neutral expression.** The system evokes investigation, travel, archives, and hidden knowledge without official marks, actor likenesses, quotes, footage, or fandom-specific hardcoding.

## Enhancement tiers

| Tier | Contract | Appropriate surfaces |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Base | Semantic HTML, static owned/placeholder imagery, CSS layout/transitions, keyboard-complete navigation | Every screen; the only required tier |
| Enhanced | Motion transitions, optimized owned video, parallax, rich charts/graphs with structured alternatives | Homepage, universe landing, relationship/timeline explorers |
| Immersive | Lazy WebGL/3D, advanced scroll chapters, optional explicitly enabled sound | Selected public hero/feature experiences only |

The user can disable ambient effects. `prefers-reduced-motion`, data-saver signals, device capability, battery/thermal constraints where available, and rendering failure automatically fall back without losing content or navigation.

## Surface tone

- **Public:** evocative, spacious, editorial, credible, with a visible skip-to-content/skip-intro path.
- **Fan application:** compact, predictable, search-forward, readable, and modestly atmospheric.
- **Contributor:** task-led and evidence-heavy, using clear workflow/status language.
- **Moderation/administration:** calm, dense, reversible where possible, and explicit about dangerous actions.

## Non-negotiable privacy language

- “Private to you” for notes, bookmarks, ratings, Journey, blocks, mutes, and notification settings.
- “Visible to Bunker members” only after membership is established.
- A private Bunker returns a generic not-found experience to unauthorized users.
- Blocked interactions use “This interaction is unavailable”; direction is never inferred or displayed.
- Moderator views never include Journey details unless a later case-scoped policy explicitly authorizes a minimal datum.
